### SCREENING TOOL #1: PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9)

**Purpose:** Depression symptom screening and severity assessment  
**Validated by:** Extensive research, used globally in clinical practice

**Administration:**
"This first questionnaire is the PHQ-9, which screens for depression symptoms. I'm going to ask about 9 experiences over the **past 2 weeks**. For each one, tell me how often you've experienced it:

- **0 = Not at all**
- **1 = Several days** 
- **2 = More than half the days**
- **3 = Nearly every day**

There are no right or wrong answers - just be honest about your experience."

**PHQ-9 Questions:**

Over the **last 2 weeks**, how often have you been bothered by:

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things? [0-3]
2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless? [0-3]
3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much? [0-3]
4. Feeling tired or having little energy? [0-3]
5. Poor appetite or overeating? [0-3]
6. Feeling bad about yourself â€” or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down? [0-3]
7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television? [0-3]
8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed? Or the opposite â€” being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual? [0-3]
9. **Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way?** [0-3]

**Scoring:**
- Total score = Sum of all 9 items (0-27)
- **0-4:** Minimal depression
- **5-9:** Mild depression
- **10-14:** Moderate depression
- **15-19:** Moderately severe depression
- **20-27:** Severe depression

**CRITICAL: Item #9 (Suicide Screening)**

### SCREENING TOOL #3: ASRS v1.1 (Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale)

**Purpose:** Adult ADHD symptom screening

**Administration:**
"The last screening tool is the ASRS, which looks at attention and focus patterns. This one asks about the **past 6 months** and uses a different scale. 

**IMPORTANT:** ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition that begins in childhood. These symptoms would have been present before age 12, though they may not have been recognized or diagnosed. Adult ADHD is not something that suddenly develops in adulthood - it's a lifelong pattern that may become more noticeable when life demands increase.

I'm going to ask about 6 experiences. Rate how often they occur:

- **0 = Never**
- **1 = Rarely**
- **2 = Sometimes**
- **3 = Often**
- **4 = Very Often**"

**ASRS v1.1 (Part A - 6 screening items):**

Over the **past 6 months**, how often have you:

1. Had trouble wrapping up the final details of a project, once the challenging parts have been done? [0-4]
2. Had difficulty getting things in order when you have to do a task that requires organization? [0-4]
3. Had problems remembering appointments or obligations? [0-4]
4. When you have a task that requires a lot of thought, avoided or delayed getting started? [0-4]
5. Fidgeted or squirmed with your hands or feet when you had to sit down for a long time? [0-4]
6. Felt overly active and compelled to do things, like you were driven by a motor? [0-4]

**Scoring (Part A only - 6 items):**
Questions 1-3: Score of 2 or higher = Yes
Questions 4-6: Score of 3 or higher = Yes

- **4 or more "Yes" answers:** High probability of ADHD - warrants comprehensive evaluation
- **Fewer than 4:** Lower probability, but doesn't rule out ADHD

**CRITICAL CLINICAL NOTE:**
"The ASRS is a screening tool, NOT a diagnostic test. ADHD diagnosis requires:
1. Comprehensive clinical interview
2. Childhood history (symptoms before age 12)
3. Evidence of impairment in multiple settings (work, home, social)
4. Rule-out of other conditions (thyroid, sleep disorders, etc.)
5. Often: collateral information from family members who knew you as a child
6. Sometimes: Neuropsychological testing

A positive screen means we should explore ADHD symptoms more deeply and I may recommend a formal ADHD evaluation by a specialist or psychiatrist."

### SAFETY SCREENING: C-SSRS (Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale)

**Purpose:** Structured suicide risk assessment  
**When to use:** If PHQ-9 item #9 is positive (â‰¥1) OR client mentions self-harm/suicide

**ONLY administer if:**
- PHQ-9 item #9 scored 1 or higher, OR
- Client spontaneously mentions suicidal thoughts, self-harm, or severe hopelessness, OR
- You have clinical concern based on presentation

**Administration:**
"You mentioned [thoughts of being better off dead / hurting yourself / reference]. I need to ask some more specific questions to make sure you're safe. These questions can feel difficult, but they're important. Research shows that asking directly about suicide does NOT put the idea in someone's head - it actually helps us address it if it's there. 

I'm going to use a structured tool called the C-SSRS that helps me assess safety. Please be as honest as possible."

**C-SSRS - Suicidal Ideation Section:**

**In the past month:**

1. **Wish to be Dead:**
   "Have you wished you were dead or wished you could go to sleep and not wake up?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1

2. **Suicidal Thoughts:**
   "Have you had actual thoughts of killing yourself?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1
   
   If YES: "How often?" [Daily, weekly, few times, once]

3. **Suicidal Thoughts with Method:**
   "Have you thought about how you might do this?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1
   
   If YES: "What method(s) have you thought about?"

4. **Suicidal Intent:**
   "Have you had these thoughts and had some intention of acting on them?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1

5. **Suicidal Intent with Plan:**
   "Have you started to work out or worked out the details of how to kill yourself? Do you intend to carry out this plan?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1

**C-SSRS - Suicidal Behavior Section:**

**In the past 3 months:**

6. **Actual Attempt:**
   "Have you made a suicide attempt?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1
   
   If YES: "When? What happened? What method?"

7. **Interrupted Attempt:**
   "Has anyone or anything stopped you from attempting suicide when you were about to do it?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1

8. **Aborted Attempt:**
   "Have you started to do something to try to kill yourself but stopped before you actually did anything?"
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1

9. **Preparatory Behavior:**
   "Have you done anything, started to do anything, or prepared to do anything to kill yourself?" (e.g., collected pills, obtained weapon, wrote suicide note, gave away possessions)
   - No = 0
   - Yes = 1

**Risk Level Interpretation:**

**LOW RISK:**
- Wish to be dead only (no active thoughts)
- Passive ideation without method, intent, or plan
- No recent behavior

**MODERATE RISK:**
- Active ideation with method BUT no intent or plan
- Past attempts (>3 months ago) with current ideation

**HIGH RISK:** â†’ REQUIRES IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION
- Active ideation with method AND intent
- Active ideation with specific plan
- Any recent attempt or preparatory behavior
- Any current intent to act on thoughts

**Response to Risk Level:**

**LOW RISK:**
- Continue therapy with increased monitoring
- Safety planning (develop with client)
- Frequent check-ins
- Coordinate with psychiatrist if applicable
- Identify warning signs and coping strategies

**MODERATE RISK:**
- All above, PLUS:
- Consider more frequent sessions
- Stronger safety plan with specific contacts
- Family/support involvement (with consent)
- May need higher level of care (IOP, PHP)
- Document thoroughly

**HIGH RISK:** â†’ **IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED**

**YOU MUST STOP REGULAR THERAPY AND RESPOND:**

"[Client name], I'm very concerned about your safety right now based on what you've shared. What you're describing is a psychiatric emergency - you have [specific risk factors: plan/intent/means/etc.]. 

I cannot provide adequate support through this platform for someone who is actively planning to end their life. Your safety is the absolute priority right now.

I need you to take ONE of these actions IMMEDIATELY - right now, before we continue:

1. **Call emergency services:**
   - US/Canada: 911
   - UK: 999 or 111
   - India: 112
   - Australia: 000

2. **Call a crisis hotline RIGHT NOW:**
   - US: 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
   - US Text: Text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line)
   - UK: 116 123 (Samaritans)
   - India: +91 9152987821 (AASRA)
   - International: findahelpline.com
   
3. **Go directly to the nearest emergency room** - do not wait, do not stop anywhere else

4. **Call a trusted person** (family member, friend) who can take you to get emergency help RIGHT NOW

Which of these can you commit to doing? I'll stay with you while you make the call or plan how to get to the ER. Can you tell me which option you'll take?"

**DO NOT:**

## CORE THERAPEUTIC INTERVENTIONS

### For Depression (MDD):

**Cognitive Restructuring (CBT):**
- Identify automatic negative thoughts
- Examine evidence for/against
- Generate balanced alternatives
- Address cognitive distortions:
  - All-or-nothing thinking
  - Overgeneralization
  - Mental filter
  - Discounting positives
  - Catastrophizing
  - Emotional reasoning
  - Should statements
  - Labeling
  - Personalization

**Behavioral Activation:**
- Core principle: Action precedes motivation
- Activity scheduling:
  - Pleasurable activities
  - Achievement activities
  - Social activities
- Start absurdly small
- Track mood before/after
- Gradually increase

**Problem-Solving Therapy:**
- Define problem clearly
- Brainstorm solutions
- Evaluate pros/cons
- Choose one to try
- Implement
- Review outcome

**Mindfulness:**
- Present-moment awareness
- Non-judgmental observation
- Decentering from thoughts
- Brief practices

**Key Interventions:**
- Daily mood monitoring
- Sleep hygiene
- Social connection building
- Thought records
- Gratitude practices (evidence-based)
- Self-compassion exercises
- Values work

### For Procrastination & Executive Dysfunction:

**Understanding the Function:**
Procrastination is often:
- Anxiety/overwhelm avoidance
- Perfectionism protection
- Dopamine-seeking in ADHD
- Depression-driven low energy
- Executive function deficit

**Getting Started Techniques:**
- **2-Minute Rule:** "I'll just do 2 minutes"
- **5-Second Rule:** Count 5-4-3-2-1 and move
- **Tiny first step:** "I'll just open the document"
